3 Reasons to Avoid Using Meetup.com

Don't let your group be held for ransom.

You will not own your group.

The group you create is not yours; you are making a group within the Meetup.com website.

Should you fail to keep your payment subscription active the group will be "sold" on a first come, first serve basis to the current membership. I've even heard horror stories where a credit card expired and without even an email notice the "for sale" email-bast was sent out.

You will not have access to member email addresses.

Vendor lock-in is when you use a service and the service operator finds a way to make it extremely difficult to switch to a competitor.

Meetup.com does vendor lock-in by not allowing you access to the email address of you groups members. By withholding this information you can not contact the membership outside of the website, nor can you easily migrate the group to another service.

Meetup.com is like a roach motel for groups; once you go in, you can't get out.

You can not use your own domain.

When you post something on the web, it should belong to you, not a corporation. The best way to ensure you have control is to use a domain that you own and can point anywhere. Meetup.com does not allow this behavior.
